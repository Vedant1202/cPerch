A perch for your Claude sessions.

cPerch shows you which Claude Code session needs you, which is still working, and which has finished — right in your menu bar. One click brings the exact window forward.

What cPerch does

cPerch turns "which window was that session in again?" into a quick look at your menu bar.

See every session

cPerch lists every Claude Code session you have running — in the terminal and in the Claude desktop app — in one place, with the ones waiting on you at the top.

Read the latest message

cPerch shows each session's most recent message inline, so you know what a session is asking or doing without opening its window.

Jump to the exact window

Click Jump and cPerch brings that session's existing window forward — the right terminal tab, or the Claude desktop app. It never opens a second copy.

Calm notifications

Choose what cPerch tells you about — a session waiting for you, an error, or a session finishing. cPerch respects Focus and Do Not Disturb, and stays quiet by default.

Hotkey and launch at login

Open cPerch from any app with ⌘⌥`, and have it start when your Mac does. Both are optional and off until you turn them on.

Built for accessibility

cPerch shows status with a shape and a color (not color alone), plus a high-contrast mode, VoiceOver labels, and support for reduced motion and transparency.

What each status means

cPerch puts every session into one of three states. Each one has its own shape and color, so you can tell them apart at a glance — even in grayscale or if you're color blind.

Needs input

The session is waiting for you — it asked a question or needs permission to continue. cPerch shows how long it has been waiting.

Running

The session is actively working — thinking, editing, or running a command. Nothing is needed from you right now.

Concluded

The session has finished, with nothing left pending. cPerch keeps it in the list for a while so you can review it, then clears it.

Your data stays on your Mac

cPerch works entirely on your own computer. It reads your local Claude files to find your sessions, and it collects nothing, sends nothing, and has no accounts, analytics, or servers.

Privacy focused

cPerch asks for no Accessibility permission, no Input Monitoring, and no login tokens. It finds your sessions straight from your files.

Read-only

cPerch only reads your local ~/.claude files to detect sessions. It never changes, writes to, or deletes anything there.

No network

cPerch makes no network connections. Your messages and project details never leave your Mac.

Get started

There's no packaged download yet, so you build cPerch from source. You only need Apple's Command Line Tools — not the full Xcode app.

Terminal 
# Clone, build, and launch cPerch
$ git clone https://github.com/Vedant1202/cPerch.git
$ cd cPerch
$ ./build.sh            # builds dist/CPerch.app
$ open dist/CPerch.app  # look for the cPerch dot, top-right
